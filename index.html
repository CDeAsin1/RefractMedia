Why Refract exists
Most news doesn't lie outright. It just shows you one angle of the light. Refract breaks complex stories into their real components — so young people can see the full spectrum of what's actually happening.
Mission

To improve media literacy in young adults by delivering rigorously fact-checked, clearly explained journalism on US politics, Peruvian affairs, and LGBTQ+ rights — without the spin.

Vision

A generation that reads the news critically, questions its sources, and understands that behind every headline is a spectrum of context, power, and truth.

The Refract Manifesto
Light looks white until you refract it. Then you see everything it was hiding.

The news works the same way. A headline is just one wavelength. Behind it is a spectrum of context, power, history, and truth that most outlets never bother to show you — because it's easier, faster, and more profitable not to.

We're not here to be faster. We're here to be clearer.

Refract exists because the stories that get told in shorthand are usually the ones that matter most. LGBTQ+ rights reduced to culture war. Latin American politics covered only when it's catastrophic. Science explained badly until people stop trusting it.

We split the light. We show you what's inside it. And we trust you to decide what to do with what you see.
